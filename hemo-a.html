Replacement therapy of factor VIII, desmopressin (an synthetic analog of 8-arginine vasopressin which is antidiuretic peptide drug) raises the levels of factor VIII in the blood from specific FVIII containing tissues and may be given directly into a vein or through a nasal spray, antifibrinolytics, which slow the breakdown of clotting factors in the blood, can also be used to treat a mild form of the disorder.
